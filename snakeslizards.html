🐍 Snakes and Lizards🦎 (Squamates)

Scincidae (Skinks)

Common 5-lined Skink (Plestiodon fasciatus)

  • Five lengthwise stripes on black (female) or dull (male) backgrounds
  • Bright blue tails when young
  • Adult males are dull brown
  • Smooth scales


Viperidae (Vipers)

Timber Rattlesnake (Crotalus horridus)

  • Black W-shaped cross bands on a tan, yellow, or dark background
  • Black tail and yellowish-tan rattle
  • May have a yellow head or black head
  • Heavily keeled scales
  • Venomous


Snakes and Lizards (Squamates)

Ringnecked Snake (Diadophis punctatus)

  • Smooth scales
  • Slender body with blue-gray back and sides
  • Yellow or orange underside
  • Yellow or orange ring around neck usually outlined in black

Eastern Milksnake (Lampropeltis triangulum)

  • Reddish-brown blotches outlined in blackl
  • White “Y” on back of head
  • White and black checkerboard pattern on underside
  • Smooth scales

Common Water Snake (Nerodia sipedon)

  • Reddish-brown blotches on a cream background
  • Darken with as they age to almost solid black or
    brown with dark brown blotchess
  • Dark vertical stripes on their lips
  • Glossy smooth underside with red crescents on a
    white or yellow background
  • Heavily keeled scales

Smooth Greensnake (Opheodrys vernalis)

  • Smooth scales
  • Solid Bright green
  • White or off-white belly
  • Neonates are very dark green
  • Turn blue when they die


Central Ratsnake (Pantherophis alleghaniensis)

  • Large Black Snake
  • Usually shows remnants of juvenile blotched pattern of
    white between scales
  • White under chin and throat, that changes to a black and
    white checkerboard pattern towards the tail,
    which is a solid dark color
  • Weakly keeled scales


DeKay’s Brownsnake (Storeria dekayi)

  • Keeled scales
  • Brown or greyish brown
  • Wide light brown stripe down the center of the back
  • Black spots that sometimes form checkerboard pattern on each side of stripe
  • White or tan underside
  • Black spot on top of head and under eye
  • Black “sideburns” on each side of neck


Red-bellied Snake (Storeria occipitomaculata)

  • Keeled scales
  • Brown but sometimes black or grey
  • Typically, two thin dark stripes along sides of their back
  • Typically, two thin dark stripes along sides of their back
  • Underside is almost always bright red that is outlined in black


Eastern Ribbonsnake (Thamnophis saurita)

  • Keeled scaled
  • Brigh white upper lip
  • Reddish brown top of head
  • Thin black line behind eyes
  • Vertical white bar in front of eye
  • Mahogany stripe along lower sides and edges of belly
  • Long tails

Common Gartersnake (Thamnophis sirtalis)

  • Yellowish upper lip
  • Dark Green on top of head
  • No black behind eyes
  • Thin black line behind eyes
  • NO Vertical white bar in front of eye
