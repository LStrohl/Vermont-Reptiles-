🐢Testudines 🐢(Turtles)

Emydidae (Pond / Marsh Turtles)

Painted Turtle (Chrysemys picta)

  • Smooth shell with red/yellow markings
  • Common in ponds and slow water

Spotted Turtle (Clemmys guttata)

  • Very small
  • Moderately domed, smooth, oval carapace
  • Black carapace with yellow spots
  • Plastron is yellow or orange with black outer edges on scutes
  • Dark skin with yellow spots

Wood Turtle (Glyptemys insculpta)

  • Moderately domed carapace with scutes that form clear concentric rings
  • Carapace is brown or gray with a weakly toothed posterior margin
  • Wide yellow plastron, with black on outer edges of scutes
  • Wide yellow plastron, with black on outer edges of scutes

Trionychidae (Soft Shell Turtles)

Spiny Soft Shell (Apalone spinifera)

  • Flat, smooth, leathery carapace with no scutes
  • Grey-brown with dark spots or rings


Chelydridae (Snapping Turtles)

North American Snapping Turtle (Chelydra serpentina)

  • Slightly domed carapace with toothed rear edge
  • Small, narrow plastron
  • Long tail with spines
  • Greyish-brown, no bright markings


Kinosternidae (Mud / Musk Turtles)

Eastern Musk Turtle (Sternotherus odoratus)

  • Oval grey/green smooth shell
  • Small hinged plastron
  • Two white head stripes
  • Musky odor when threatened
